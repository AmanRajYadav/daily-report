What We Explored

Literature: Unlocking the Secrets of the Mind

Today's adventure was a deep dive into the human mind! We started by asking a powerful question: 'What makes someone go mad?' We used the story of 'Clovis and Jane' to discover how authors explore this idea. We explored how invisible forces, like social pressure, can feel like a trap (just like feeling you have to act a certain way around different groups of friends). The coolest part was realizing that stories like this are 'thought experiments' that help us understand ourselves and the world better. This connects to our future lessons on character development and helps you build empathy, a real-life superpower.

Your Mission

🎯 The Pressure-Poem Mission

Your mission: Think about a time you felt a small, invisible pressure (like the pressure to get a question right, or to be good at a game). Step 1: Grab a piece of paper and write down 3 words that describe that feeling. Step 2: Write a short, 4-line poem using at least two of those words. Step 3: Draw a small symbol next to your poem that represents the feeling. Bonus challenge: Try writing a second poem from the opposite perspective – the person or thing creating the pressure!

✨ Why it's amazing:

This activity is amazing because it connects a big idea from a story to your own life, which helps your brain remember it forever. You'll develop your creative expression and emotional intelligence while having fun! Plus, you can share your poem and see if your family has ever felt the same way.
